SaaS Strategy

How to Evaluate SaaS Platforms: A Buyer's Guide

MT
Maya Torres
Jan 12, 2026 · 7 min read

Choosing the right SaaS platform is one of the most impactful decisions a team lead or operations manager can make. The wrong choice means months of migration pain, lost productivity, and frustrated employees. The right choice can transform how your organization works. Start by defining your must-haves versus nice-to-haves. Security certifications like SOC 2 and GDPR compliance should be non-negotiable. Evaluate the vendor's uptime track record — anything below 99.9% should raise red flags for business-critical software. Look beyond feature checklists and request a hands-on trial. How intuitive is the interface for non-technical users? What does the onboarding process look like? How responsive is customer support during the trial period? Pay close attention to pricing models: per-user pricing can balloon quickly as your team grows.

The ROI of Workflow Automation for Small Teams

JP
Jordan Patel
Jan 5, 2026 · 6 min read

Small teams often assume workflow automation is a luxury reserved for enterprises with dedicated IT departments. The reality is exactly the opposite — smaller teams have the most to gain because every hour saved has a proportionally larger impact. Consider a team of ten where three people spend 30 minutes daily on manual task routing, status updates, and notification management. That's 7.5 hours per day — nearly a full-time employee's worth of productivity lost to process overhead. With automation, those tasks happen instantly and accurately. At CloudSync, we've tracked real customer data and found that small teams (5-25 people) recover an average of 15 hours per week through workflow automation. At a blended hourly rate of $50, that's $39,000 in annual savings — far exceeding the cost of most automation tools.
