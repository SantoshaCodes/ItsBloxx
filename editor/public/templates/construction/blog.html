How to Choose a General Contractor You Can Trust

DT
Diana Torres
Jan 12, 2026 ¬∑ 8 min read

Hiring a general contractor is a decision that will affect your home, your budget, and your daily life for weeks or months. Unfortunately, the construction industry has its share of unreliable operators, which makes choosing the right partner critical. Start by verifying their license and insurance ‚Äî in Colorado, contractors must carry general liability and workers' compensation. Ask for at least three references from recent projects similar to yours, and actually call them. Visit a current job site if possible to see how the crew works and whether the site is clean and organized. Get at least three written bids, but don't automatically choose the cheapest ‚Äî a lowball bid often means corners will be cut or change orders will pile up. Look for a contractor who communicates clearly, provides a detailed written contract with a payment schedule, and is willing to pull all necessary permits. Trust your gut: if something feels off during the sales process, it will only get worse during construction.

The Complete Guide to Building Permits

RN
Ryan Nguyen
Jan 5, 2026 ¬∑ 6 min read

Building permits are one of the most misunderstood aspects of construction, yet they exist to protect you. A permit ensures that your project meets local building codes for structural integrity, fire safety, electrical systems, and plumbing. In Denver, you generally need a permit for any work that changes the structure, electrical, plumbing, or mechanical systems of a building. This includes additions, deck construction, finishing a basement, moving walls, and upgrading electrical panels. Minor cosmetic work like painting, flooring, and cabinet refacing typically does not require a permit. The permit process usually takes 2-6 weeks depending on project complexity. Your contractor should handle the application, but you as the homeowner are ultimately responsible. Skipping permits can result in fines, required demolition of unpermitted work, difficulty selling your home, and insurance claims being denied. At Summit Builders, we handle every permit application and inspection as part of our standard project management process.
