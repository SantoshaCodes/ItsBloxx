Business Insurance

5 Types of Insurance Every Small Business Needs

RC
Robert Chen
Jan 20, 2026 ¬∑ 7 min read

Starting a business is exciting, but without the right insurance, one unexpected event could put everything at risk. After 25 years of helping small business owners in Texas, we've seen firsthand how the right coverage can mean the difference between a minor setback and financial ruin. Every small business, regardless of industry, should carry these five essential policies: general liability insurance to protect against third-party claims, professional liability (errors and omissions) for service-based businesses, commercial property insurance for your physical assets, workers' compensation if you have employees, and business interruption insurance to cover lost income during disasters. The cost of these policies varies by industry, revenue, and number of employees, but most small businesses can secure a comprehensive package for $1,000 to $3,000 per year. The key is working with an independent agent who can compare quotes from multiple carriers rather than settling for the first offer.

How to Lower Your Home Insurance Premium

AT
Angela Torres
Jan 12, 2026 ¬∑ 6 min read

Home insurance is a necessity, but that doesn't mean you have to overpay. Many homeowners are paying hundreds more than they need to simply because they haven't reviewed their policies in years. The most effective way to save is bundling your home and auto insurance with the same carrier, which can reduce your premium by 15 to 25 percent. Increasing your deductible from $500 to $1,000 can save another 10 to 15 percent, though you should only do this if you have enough savings to cover the higher out-of-pocket cost. Home security systems, smoke detectors, and deadbolt locks can qualify you for safety discounts. If your home is older, upgrading your roof, plumbing, or electrical system can also lower your rate. Ask your agent about loyalty discounts, claims-free discounts, and whether your credit score is impacting your premium. Finally, shop around every two to three years because rates vary significantly between carriers for the same coverage.

Understanding Umbrella Insurance: Do You Need It?

DP
David Park
Jan 5, 2026 ¬∑ 8 min read

Umbrella insurance is one of the most misunderstood yet valuable types of coverage available. It provides an extra layer of liability protection above and beyond your existing home, auto, and other policies. If you're ever sued for damages that exceed your standard policy limits, an umbrella policy kicks in to cover the difference. Consider this scenario: you cause a serious car accident and the injured party sues for $1.5 million, but your auto policy only covers $500,000. Without umbrella insurance, you'd be personally responsible for the remaining $1 million, potentially putting your home, savings, and future earnings at risk. Umbrella policies typically start at $1 million in coverage and cost between $150 and $300 per year, making them one of the most affordable forms of insurance relative to the protection they provide. Anyone with significant assets, a swimming pool, a teenage driver, or a dog should seriously consider an umbrella policy. It's not just for the wealthy.
