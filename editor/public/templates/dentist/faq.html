Common Questions

Everything You Want to Know

Can't find what you're looking for? Contact us and we'll be happy to help.

We recommend visiting the dentist every six months for a routine checkup and professional cleaning. However, patients with gum disease, a history of cavities, or other oral health conditions may need more frequent visits. During your checkup, we'll assess your individual needs and recommend an appropriate schedule.

Yes, we accept most major dental insurance plans including Delta Dental, Cigna, Aetna, MetLife, and many others. Our front desk team will verify your coverage before your appointment and help you understand your benefits. For patients without insurance, we offer affordable payment plans and a membership discount program.

Call our office immediately at (512) 555-0234. We offer same-day emergency appointments for issues like severe toothaches, knocked-out teeth, broken teeth, and lost fillings. If a tooth is knocked out, place it in milk and get to our office within 30 minutes for the best chance of saving it. For after-hours emergencies, our voicemail provides instructions for reaching our on-call dentist.

Invisalign is effective for most mild to moderate orthodontic issues including crowding, spacing, overbites, underbites, and crossbites. The best way to find out is to schedule a free Invisalign consultation. We'll take digital scans of your teeth, show you a 3D preview of your expected results, and discuss whether clear aligners or traditional braces would be the best option for your specific case.

Professional teeth whitening results typically last between one and three years, depending on your diet and oral hygiene habits. Foods and beverages like coffee, tea, red wine, and berries can stain teeth over time. We recommend using a whitening toothpaste, avoiding staining foods immediately after treatment, and scheduling periodic touch-up treatments to maintain your bright smile.

We understand that dental anxiety is real and common. Our office offers several comfort options including nitrous oxide (laughing gas), oral sedation for moderate anxiety, noise-canceling headphones, warm blankets, and a calming office environment. We also take time to explain every procedure beforehand and establish a stop signal so you always feel in control during your visit.

The cost of dental implants varies depending on the complexity of your case, the number of implants needed, and whether additional procedures like bone grafting are required. A single implant typically ranges from $3,000 to $5,000 including the crown. We offer complimentary implant consultations and flexible financing options to make this life-changing treatment accessible.

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends scheduling your child's first dental visit by their first birthday or within six months of their first tooth erupting. Early visits help us monitor development, catch potential issues early, and establish a positive relationship between your child and dental care. Our team is experienced in making young patients feel comfortable and even excited about their visits.
